Thiruvananthapuram

43rd Annual day has been celebrated on 06.07.2014 at Vailoppalli Samskrithi Bhavan, Near Nandancode in association with Bharat Bhavan, Dept. of Culture, Govt. of Kerala.

The long felt desire of Telugu knowing public of Thiruvananthapuram to form a cultural association was fulfilled on the 11th January 1971, when Telugu Samskrithika Sangham was registered under the Travancore - Cochin Literary, Scientific and Charitable Societies Act XII of 1955.

The Telugu Sangham has become a platform for its members to express themselves in various art forms like music, drama, dance and literature. Sangham has organised over fifty dramas; equal number of classical, folk and film musical concerts; multitude poetry recitals; Rangavalli displays and Telugu food festivals -all ardently participated by all the members.

The Sangham while identifying itself with glorious Telugu culture is first and foremost to cut the barriers of language and region and integrate itself with all cultural organizations in Thiruvananthapuram to promote National Integration and espouse splendid Indian culture. The Sangham is a proud member of Bharat Bhavan, Department of Culture, Government of Kerala, ever since its inception and worked together to organise numerous cultural programmes.
