About

The long felt desire of Telugu knowing public of Thiruvananthapuram to form a cultural association was fulfilled on the 11th January 1971, when Telugu Samskrithika Sangham was registered under the Travancore - Cochin Literary, Scientific and Charitable Societies Act XII of 1955.

The Telugu Sangham has become a platform for its members to express themselves in various art forms like music, drama, dance and literature. Sangham has organised over fifty dramas; equal number of classical, folk and film musical concerts; multitude poetry recitals; Rangavalli displays and Telugu food festivals -all ardently participated by all the members.

The Sangham while identifying itself with glorious Telugu culture is first and foremost to cut the barriers of language and region and integrate itself with all cultural organizations in Thiruvananthapuram to promote National Integration and espouse splendid Indian culture. The Sangham is a proud member of Bharat Bhavan, Department of Culture, Government of Kerala, ever since its inception and worked together to organise numerous cultural programmes.

History

Necessity is the mother of Invention and Telugu Samskrithika Sangham (TSS) sprouted out from this sheer necessity. It was at this point, when few valiant Telugu speaking people took the onerous responsibility to set the things right and formed TSS with their own financial contribution.

The deep down belief and the respect for the Telugu culture gave birth to TSS in 1971. The task to build up an association like this, thus the journey of TSS started. Today, with every passing year, we are growing stronger, beaming alive with cultural ethos. With such a noble initiative, we have marched forward and are contributing and executing future plans to volunteer for several social causes The Sangham has also screened over hundred Telugu films and organised four week long Film Festivals. The pinnacle of shows was nine day (Nava Rathri Navonmesham) long cultural extravaganza of music and dance with the theme of ‘Unity by celebration of Culture and Heritage’.

The Sangham had also the honour of presenting to enlightened citizens of Thiruvananthapuram some of the greatest artists and scholars from Andhra Pradesh. Gana gandharva Balamurali Krishna, Sri Emani Sankara Satry, Sri Vempati China Sathyam, Smt.Sobha Naidu, Sri Kala Krishna, Sri Chintha Ramanadham, Sri Nerella Venumadhav, Sri B.V Pattabhiram, Dr. C.Narayana Reddy, Sri Gollapudi Maruthi Rao, Dr. Gowri Shankar, Kala Tapaswi K. Viswanath and Dr. Akkineni Nageswara Rao are only few among them who have enthralled the audience with mesmerising expositions in Classical Music; Kuchipudi and Perini Natyam; Literature; Mimicry and Indrajal. The Sangham had also distinction of hosting several Kerala art forms including performances by renowned Kerala Kalamandalam.
